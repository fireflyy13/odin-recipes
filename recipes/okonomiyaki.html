Okonomiyaki

Description

Okonomiyaki (お好み焼き) is a popular pan-fried dish that consists of batter and cabbage.

  • Nagaimo
  • Tenkasu
  • Okonomiyaki Sauce
  • Japanese Mayonnaise
  • Katsuobushi
  • Aonori

Steps

  1. After one hour, take out the batter from the refrigerator. Add 4 large eggs (50 g each w/o

    shell), ½ cup tenkasu/agedama (tempura scraps), and ¼ cup pickled red ginger (beni

    shoga or kizami beni shoga) to the bowl. Mix until combined.

  2. Add the minced cabbage to the batter, one-third of it at a time. Mix well before adding the

    rest.

  3. Prepare a large griddle or two large frying pans with lids to cook two savory pancakes at a

    time. In the pans, heat a bit of neutral oil on medium heat. When it‘s hot (400ºF or 200ºC),

    spread 1 portion of the batter, about 6 inches (15 cm) in diameter, in a circle in each pan. We

    like thicker okonomiyaki (the final thickness is ¾ inches or 2 cm). If you’re new to making

    okonomiyaki, make it smaller and thinner so it’s easier to flip.

  4. Place 3 slices of pork belly on top of each okonomiyaki and cook covered on medium-low

    heat for 5 minutes.

  5. When the bottom side is nicely browned, flip them over.

  6. Gently press the okonomiyaki to fix their shape and keep them together. Cover and cook for

    another 5 minutes.

  7. Flip them over one last time and cook uncovered for 2 minutes. Transfer the cooked

    okonomiyaki to a plate. Continue cooking the rest of the okonomiyaki.
